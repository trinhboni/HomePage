Forge Media and Design's Interactive Team has given me a huge opportunity to be apart of working in the research and early design process of the Ground-Breaking Project.

GOALS

The goal of the project is to craft an artful installation to add a vibrant sense of life to greatly enhance the tenant and visitor experience while creatively capturing the essence of their brand – growth.

-- ideations --

RESEARCH DEVELOPMENT

PRIMARY RESEARCH  
CONTEXTUAL INQUIRY

Before we actually jumped into iterations and doing the design process, the team decided to meet up at the Globe and Mail building for scouting and figuring out the foot traffic of the space.

Contextual Inquiry is the observation method that our team used to obtain information about the space from the real users. The goal is to watch how test participants interact within the space (Globe and Mail’s entrance) and research the  architectural layout of the lobby.

SECONDARY RESEARCH
- 01 - INTERSTITIAL SPACE

After multiple meeting and iterations, we have decided to pick 2 themes/concepts for the installation.
‍
We call the first one INTERSTITIAL SPACE. It is an exploration of the symbiotic relationship between the city and it's citizens. The piece actively translates the momentum of the lobby’s pedestrians, adding colourful lines that weave throughout the illustrated cityscape, revealing depth and imbuing energy into the art.

SECONDARY RESEARCH
- 02 - "BLOOM"

Bloom invites the viewer to wander into its idyllic, hand-illustrated forest scenes to enjoy a moment of respite.

It highlights the importance of maintaining a balance between nature and the urban sprawl, so that we can continue to find solace within its warm embrace.
