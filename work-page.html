SIX WORD STORY

INTRODUCTION

The goal of this project was to be able to tell a story with only 6 words through motion graphics. I created a minute long animation that tells a story based on the 6 words:

" When Korean Culture Soothes The Seoul "

This short animation is telling the story of a traveler who walks through Seoul City and what he experiences and learns through his trip.

IDEATION

I knew that for this video I wanted to focus on the experience of Korean culture. Because of this the first word I decided to focus on was “SEOUL.” Seoul it is the name of the capital city of South Korea and it also it has same pronunciation as the word“SOUL” which I think it is a good concept to play with word. The “SOUL/SEOUL” in this video is describing the soul of the people who travel through the city.

The box that is placed on his shirt represents the traveler's “soul." In the video as soon as he begins walking, the box opens which means the traveler is opening his soul to a new world.  

The heart that hovers above the traveler represents his “LOVE LEVEL.” Every time he passes and experiences new scenery in Seoul the heart fills up a certain amount which shows that the more he travels the more he falls in love with the city. When the heart fills up, it flies back to the box which represents that the memories the traveler gained will always be there in his soul/heart.

WORKING WITH ILLUSTRATOR

After I sketched all of the elements that I needed, I moved all the sketches to Illustrator and began tracing the character as a vector, so it can be animated in After Effects.

[ PROCESS ]

WORKING WITh character animator

For character animation, I tested out many programs and plugins like After Effect puppet tool, Flash Animate CC and Adobe Character Animator CC to find out which would be the most efficient and can provide me with a natural walking cycle. Adobe Character Animator worked the best because it saved me a lot of time and it is very convenient when it comes to fixing the person walking style.

Here are the steps that I went through working with Character Animator CC:

Step 1 : Create multilayer with different body parts for my character.

Step 2 : Create a puppet using rig to pin each body parts.

Step 3 : Fix the walking behavior with the control bar.

Step 4 : Record the puppet's behavior.

[ PROCESS ]

WORKING WITh after effect

To create a smooth animation, I needed to split each illustration into multiple layers and work with each layer in After Effects.
